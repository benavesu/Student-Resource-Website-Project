Online Programming Resources



A Couple of Links:

Below are links related to programming that can provide more information students can build upon throughout the semester. Some of these links provide practice problems, projects, videos, etc. This is not to be used to replace your courses but to reinforce the skills you are currently learning:

  1. 1. W3schools: Provides free programming practice for both desktop and mobile.
    • https://www.w3schools.com/
  1. 2. Geeks4Geeks:​​ A Computer Science portal for "computer science enthusiasts". It contains well written, well thought and well explained computer sciences and programming articles, quizzes and more.
    • https://​www.geeksforgeeks.org
    • ​Geeks4Geeks Youtube Channel
  1. 3. TutorialsPoint: Originated from the idea that there exists a class of readers who respond better to online content and prefer to learn new skills at their own pace from the comforts of their drawing rooms. Mission is to deliver Simply Easy Learning with clear, crisp, and to-the-point content on a wide range of technical and non-technical subjects without any preconditions and impediments.
    • ​Computer Programming
    • ​Computer Science
    • ​Web Development
    • ​For more tutorials...
  1. 4. Stacks Overflow: Provides the largest, most trusted online community for developers to learn, share​ ​their programming ​knowledge, and build their careers.
    • ​Stacks Overflow Site
  1. 5. Jetbrains Academy: Provides projects users can play around with in Python, Java, Kotlin, etc. Users can log in utilizing their Github, Google, or Facebook account.
    • To sign up or log in: Jetbrains Signup/Login Page

    • Choose a language of choice and difficulty, work at your own pace.
  1. 6. CodeWars: Improve skills by training with others in real code challenges.
    • To sign up: CodeWars Signup Page

    • To login: CodeWars Login Page
  1. 7. SoloLearn​: Provides free programming practice for both desktop and mobile for many programming languages.
    • Start SoloLearn
  1. 8. HackersRank​: Provides challenges and practice in programming skills, and you can move up in ranks the more you practice.
    • HackersRank
  1. 9. MIT OpenCourseWare | Free Online Course Materials​: MIT OpenCourseWare is a web-based publication of virtually all MIT course content. OCW is open and available to the world and is a permanent MIT activity.
    • MIT Course Tools
  1. 10. ​Tim Roughgarden Lectures: Informational videos pertaining to data structures and algorithms.
    • Tim Youtube Lectures
  1. 11. Stanford Open Classroom: Full courses, short videos, free for everyone with regard to --> Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction Design, Practical Unix, Design and Analysis of Algorithms, Introduction to Databases, Unsupervised Feature Learning and Deep Learning, Discrete Probability, Machine Learning, and Algebra One.
    • Open Classroom
  1. 12. Github: A free web-based platform used for version control. Git simplifies the process of working with other people and makes it easy to collaborate on projects. Team members can work on files and easily merge their changes in with the master branch of the project.
    • GitHub Link
